My Son or Daughter Won't Go to Rehab — What Now?

TheFirstWord

You finally said it. You brought up rehab, treatment, getting help — and they said no. Maybe they got angry. Maybe they shut down. Maybe they just walked away.

This is normal, and it doesn't mean you failed. Refusal isn't the end of the conversation — it's usually just the first round of it.

Don't escalate into an ultimatum you're not ready to keep

"Get help or I'm done with you" only works if you actually mean it and are prepared to follow through. An empty ultimatum teaches them your boundaries aren't real, which makes the next conversation harder, not easier.

Separate the relationship from the behavior

You can keep loving your child while refusing to fund, excuse, or clean up after the addiction. This isn't contradictory — it's actually the most stable position you can hold long-term.

Figure out what "no" actually means

"No" to a 28-day inpatient program is different from "no" to all forms of help. Sometimes the real no is about cost, about fear of withdrawal, about not wanting to lose their job, or about not trusting treatment programs in general. Find the specific no underneath the general no — it's usually more workable. If what you’re actually hitting is denial that there’s any problem at all, that’s a different conversation — here’s what to say when they won’t admit it.

Have a Plan B ready, not just a Plan A you keep repeating

If "go to rehab" didn't land, the next move isn't repeating the same ask louder. It's a different, smaller ask — outpatient counselling, a single call to a helpline, attending one Al-Anon-adjacent meeting, or simply agreeing to talk to a doctor about it. Smaller asks get said yes to far more often. When you’re ready to reopen the subject, this guide on bringing up their drinking without a blow-up covers how to frame round two.

Don't go it alone

Whatever you decide to say or do next, having an actual support structure — even just one person who understands what you're dealing with — matters more than most families expect.
